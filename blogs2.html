Importance of Cyber Security

Our world today is ruled by technology and we can't do without it at all. From booking our flight tickets, to catching up with an old friend, technology plays an important role in it. However, the same technology may expose you when it's vulnerable and could lead to loss of essential data. Cyber security, alongside physical commercial security has thus, slowly and steadily, become one of the most important topics in the business industry to be talked about. Seeing the increasing use of electronic gadgets now a day, everyone needs to have proper cybersecurity. With its help, we ensure the safety of our private data and official and personal documents. However in the absence of Cyber Security, anyone can steal our data and misuse them for some illegal purposes.

In India, An average of 1,500 cybercrime instances are reported every day, but only about 30 are processed into FIRs. These numbers were part of the information supplied by the Ministry of Home Affairs (MHA) in Lok Sabha. As per data of statista, around 2,50,000 cases was registered from year 2012 to 2021 and it is constently increasing every year. From it 59% of adults are victim. Womens are most commonly exposed to the cyber crimes like Sextortion, Phishing, Pornography, Cyber stalking, Cyber-bullying, Cyber hacking. In which hackers use victims' private photos or altered images as blackmail, the offenders started demanding money or sexual favors from them. Some hackers send fack e-mails with a link to a particular webpage in an effort to coerce the victim into entering personal information like contact details and passwords or with the purpose of infecting the victim's device with dangerous viruses as soon as the link is clicked. These texts and emails appear to be authentic. The attackers then carry out shady transactions from the victim's bank account to their own using the victim's bank account and other private information.

Hackers often targets bank accounts.Everyone looks to be entirely cashless and using digital payment methods like debit and credit cards. In this case, ensuring that the required cybersecurity safeguards are in place to protect your privacy and data is critical.The majority of the time, when a bank's data is compromised, you lose time and money. Recovery from the same can be unpleasant and time-consuming. It would entail canceling cards, reviewing statements, and keeping a watchful lookout for issues.

If we want to protect our data we should take some prevention. By keeping software up-to-date, avoid opening suspicious emails, use Antivirus and Antimalware software on the electronic devices, use two-factor authentication, use strong passwords and dont reuse passwords.
